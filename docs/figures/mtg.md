# Marjorie Taylor Greene — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: new-2

Greene ends 2025 as the GOP's loudest populist defector: still hard-right on
social issues (her trans-care felony ban passed the House in December 2025),
but openly at war with Trump over the Epstein files, tariffs/affordability,
foreign wars, and H-1B visas, resigning her seat effective January 2026. Her x
stays firmly right on culture; her y is maximally insurgent — she now indicts
both parties, the donor class, the media, the FBI, and once called for
"national divorce."

## Per-question evidence

"It shouldn't be about helping your crypto donors, or your AI donors." — interview, 2025 (https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/marjorie-taylor-greene-torches-trump-144246262.html)
Long an America-First trade supporter, in 2025 she blasted the tariffs' effects on manufacturers and "regular people's bank accounts" — genuinely mixed record.

### e02 · score −1
"[Greene called] for action to address the expiring subsidies for those with health insurance plans through the Affordable Care Act." — The Hill/Newsweek coverage, Nov 2025 (https://www.newsweek.com/marjorie-taylor-greene-floats-bill-to-end-h-1b-visas-11045491)
She broke with the GOP to demand ACA-subsidy extension (see also https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/24/politics/marjorie-taylor-greene-trump-epstein-aca-affordability-analysis), but has never supported a federal guarantee/single-payer — softened opposition, not support.

### e03 · score −1
"Republicans have ZERO plans to cut social security." — X post, via Newsweek (https://www.newsweek.com/marjorie-taylor-greene-issues-warning-over-social-security-11555706)
No wealth-tax support anywhere in her record; she votes for GOP tax-cut packages (voted for the 2025 reconciliation tax bill). Opposition is consistent but she now aims her fire at "rich powerful men," so −1 not −2.

### e04 · score 0
No direct statement or clean floor vote on the federal minimum wage found in her tenure (the Raise the Wage Act predates her). Her free-market rhetoric implies opposition to mandates, but the record is silent on this exact statement.

### e05 · score +2
"[She wants to break up the companies] to allow for more freedom of speech, more information, and more competition in the marketplace." — Gettr post, Feb 2022, via Newsweek (https://www.newsweek.com/marjorie-taylor-greene-says-republicans-must-stop-big-tech-censorship-1679671)
