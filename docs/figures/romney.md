# Mitt Romney — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: verify-3

Retired from the Senate in January 2025, Romney remains the archetypal
institutionalist business conservative: pro-trade ("I'm not a tariff guy"),
pro-market but open to targeted federal programs (child allowance, $10
minimum wage with E-Verify), socially conservative with a pragmatic streak,
and the only senator ever to vote twice to convict a president of his own
party. His y-axis placement is driven by emphatic, acted-on defense of
elections, courts, the FBI, and cross-party compromise.

## Per-question evidence
### e01 · score −2
"I'm not a tariff guy... the aggressive way President Trump is imposing tariffs is wrong." — SMU appearance, Oct 2025 (https://smudailycampus.com/1067405/news/mitt-romney-reflects-on-life-after-politics-government-shutdown-the-republican-partys-future/)
Repeated 2018–2025 opposition; called tariffs "a tax on Americans" and inflationary, excepting only coordinated pressure on China (https://thehill.com/blogs/blog-briefing-room/news/395851-romney-rips-trump-tariffs-trade-wars-at-a-tax-on-americans/).

### e02 · score −1
Opposed Medicare for All and single-payer throughout his Senate term, favoring market-based, state-flexible coverage. — Senate record, 2019–2025 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Consistently against a federal guarantee, but not a repeal absolutist — he defends pre-existing-condition protections; hedged opposition, not crusading.

### e03 · score −2
Opposed the 2021 Democratic billionaires' unrealized-gains tax and every wealth-tax proposal of his career. — Senate record (https://www.romney.senate.gov/issues/taxes)
His own tax page celebrates rate cuts and competitiveness; taxing wealth stocks is squarely against his repeatedly stated framework.

### e04 · score −1
Co-sponsored the Higher Wages for American Workers Act raising the federal minimum wage to $10 with mandatory E-Verify. — Feb 2021 (https://www.romney.senate.gov/romney-cotton-colleagues-introduce-bill-to-raise-minimum-wage/)
He acted for a (modest) government-set wage floor, so he disagrees with the statement — but the low target and E-Verify pairing keep it at −1.

### e05 · score −1
Criticized Big Tech's power but pursued content/security remedies (TikTok divestiture, Section 230 scrutiny), never antitrust breakup. — Senate record (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Stated skepticism of breakup as a remedy; hedged disagreement.

### e06 · score 0
Pushed the bipartisan TRUST Act rescue-committee process for Social Security rather than privatization; his 2012 openness to personal accounts was never revived. — Senate record (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Record is genuinely mixed/silent on individual accounts in the 2019–2025 window; scored 0.

### e07 · score +1
Proposed the Family Security Act — a federal monthly child allowance of up to $350/child. — Feb 2021 (https://www.romney.senate.gov/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/family-security-act_one-pager.pdf)
A real federal family-benefit program from his own desk, though it is a cash allowance rather than paid leave/child care per se, and paired with offsets — +1.

### e08 · score −1
"By fortifying U.S. anti-money laundering frameworks, our legislation cracks down on crypto-facilitated crimes." — CANSEE Act release, Jul 2023 (https://www.romney.senate.gov/romney-colleagues-introduce-crypto-anti-money-laundering-bill-to-stop-illicit-transfers/)
He legislated to apply bank-style AML law to DeFi — the opposite of leaving crypto alone; targeted rather than comprehensive, so −1.

### e09 · score −1
Opposed the PRO Act and organized-labor's agenda; backed alternatives like employee-involvement organizations. — Senate record (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Standard Republican skepticism of unions, but no crusade against them; hedged disagreement.

### e10 · score +2
"Sad to see what's being done to bribe the voters." — X post, Aug 2022 (https://www.deseret.com/utah/2022/8/24/23320337/mitt-romney-comments-on-biden-student-loan-forgiveness)
Repeatedly called cancellation a "bribe" that "foots taxpayers with other people's financial obligations" and "is unfair to those who paid their own way" — emphatic agreement.

### e11 · score +1
Endorsed "a price on carbon... with border adjustment taxes" as the most effective climate tool. — 2022–2023 remarks (https://www.eenews.net/articles/romneys-climate-legacy-a-champion-with-few-results/)
Repeatedly voiced carbon-tax support and named climate a "critical challenge," but never signed onto carbon-pricing legislation — support stayed rhetorical, so +1 not +2.

### e12 · score +1
"I would have supported the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act as it... made our corporate tax rate competitive." — Senate taxes page (https://www.romney.senate.gov/issues/taxes)
Explicit endorsement of corporate-rate cuts as pro-worker competitiveness policy; rhetorical (he wasn't seated for the vote), so +1.

### s01 · score −1
Describes himself as pro-life with exceptions for rape, incest, and life of the mother; welcomed Dobbs returning abortion to the states. — Senate record (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Disagrees that abortion should be legal in most circumstances, but supports exceptions and never championed a national ban — −1.

### s02 · score +1
"Boys should be competing with boys and girls should be competing with (girls) on the athletic field." — Senate hearing, Feb 2021 (https://www.deseret.com/utah/2021/2/3/22265240/mitt-romney-thinks-oppose-transgender-women-competing-girls-sports-rand-paul-mike-lee-education/)
Opposed the Equality Act over gender-identity provisions (https://www.washingtonblade.com/2021/02/16/romney-declares-opposition-to-lgbtq-equality-act/); binary-sex framing is stated but not a legislative priority — +1.

### s03 · score −1
Voted for the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act (enhanced checks, red-flag funding). — Senate vote, Jun 2022 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
A pro-gun-rights Republican who nonetheless acted for new federal restrictions — he rejects the absolutist framing of the statement, −1.

### s04 · score −1
Opposed the defund-the-police movement while supporting policing reform after marching in a 2020 racial-justice protest. — 2020–2024 record (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Disagrees with redirecting police budgets, but from a reformist rather than combative posture — −1.

### s05 · score −1
His immigration bill raises "civil and criminal penalties on employers that hire unauthorized aliens" — enforcement via E-Verify, not roundups. — Feb 2021 (https://www.romney.senate.gov/romney-cotton-colleagues-introduce-bill-to-raise-minimum-wage/)
Supports interior enforcement but has long criticized indiscriminate tactics (family separation); rejects "whatever it takes" — −1.

### s06 · score +1
Long advocated expanding high-skilled legal immigration ("staple a green card" to STEM diplomas) while conditioning on border security. — career position (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
Stated, consistent support for more legal immigration, but rarely a lead issue for him — +1.

### s07 · score −1

Acknowledges racism but rejects the institutional-racism curriculum framing — hedged disagreement.

### s08 · score +1
"Freedom requires religion just as religion requires freedom." — Faith in America address, 2007 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Mitt_Romney)
A devout Latter-day Saint who consistently frames public morality in religious terms; older evidence fills a gap the 2024–26 record leaves quiet — +1.

### s09 · score 0

### s10 · score −2

### s11 · score +1

Acted to expand capital punishment; strongly disagrees with abolition — −2.
