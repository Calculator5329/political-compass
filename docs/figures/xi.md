# Xi Jinping — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: verify-10

Scored per METHOD.md's non-US-figures rule: US-framed statements are answered by
the closest analogue in Xi's governance record, noted per item. His placement is
driven by party-led state capitalism (retaliatory tariffs and self-reliance
doctrine, the tech crackdown, "common prosperity" pressure on high incomes,
universal basic medical insurance, total crypto ban, party-only unions, new
national childcare subsidies) fused with a hard security state — world-leading
executions, sinicized religion, mandatory patriotic education — and the
authoritarian pole on every system item: no competitive elections,
party-subordinated courts and media, crushed protest, term limits abolished in
2018.

## Per-question evidence

### e01 · score +2
"one of only two countries … to impose counter-tariffs on U.S. goods" — China Leadership Monitor, 2025 (https://www.prcleader.org/post/china-projects-confidence-in-the-face-of-trump-s-tariff-storm)
US analogue: tariffs to shield domestic industry. Xi answered the trade war with counter-tariffs and rare-earth export controls, and made "self-reliance" the organizing principle of national strategy — repeated, acted on, costs accepted.

### e02 · score +1
"maintained a coverage rate of around 95 percent, with over 1.32 billion people enrolled" — gov.cn, Jul 2025 (https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202507/24/content_WS6881c72cc6d0868f4e8f463b.html)
US analogue: state-guaranteed insurance for all. Xi's state runs near-universal basic medical insurance and Healthy China 2030 — but coverage is shallow with high out-of-pocket costs and hukou gaps, so agreement is real yet hedged.

### e03 · score +1
"regulate excessively high incomes and encourage high-income people … to return more to society" — Xi via Xinhua, CNBC, Aug 2021 (https://www.cnbc.com/2021/08/18/chinas-xi-emphasizes-common-prosperity-at-finance-economy-meeting.html)
US analogue: wealth tax on great fortunes. Xi's common-prosperity drive extracted billions in "voluntary" pledges from billionaires, but he never enacted a standing wealth or property tax and softened the campaign after 2022 — stated support, partially acted, hedged.

### e04 · score −2
"required to adjust their levels at least once every two years" — China Briefing on minimum wage rules (https://www.china-briefing.com/news/minimum-wages-china/)
Statement: market, not government, should set the minimum wage. Under Xi every province sets statutory minimums by regulation; 27 of 31 provinces raised them in 2025 as deliberate policy — sustained government wage-setting.

### e05 · score +2
"Xi scuttled the planned $37 billion initial public offering of Jack Ma's Ant Group" — NBC News, 2021 (https://www.nbcnews.com/news/world/china-created-more-billionaires-u-s-now-it-cracking-down-n1278438)
US analogue: breaking up the largest tech companies. Xi's crackdown killed the Ant IPO, forced its restructuring, fined Alibaba $2.8B, and erased ~$1T in tech value under new anti-monopoly rules — the statement enacted, and then some.

### e06 · score −1
State pensions remain the party-run core; Xi's 2022 "third-pillar" private accounts are a small voluntary supplement, not a carve-out of the state system (context: https://www.brookings.edu/articles/assessing-chinas-common-prosperity-campaign/)
US analogue: diverting Social Security into individual accounts. Xi keeps the mandatory system fully state-run; the voluntary pilot shows openness at the margin only — disagreement, hedged.

### e07 · score +2
"families with children under three will receive an annual subsidy of 3,600 yuan per child" — SCMP, Jul 2025 (https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3319872/china-launches-first-national-childcare-subsidies-bid-tackle-demographic-crisis)
US analogue: federal paid leave and child care. Xi's government launched the first nationwide childcare subsidy, doubled child tax deductions, and every province extended parental leave — a national family-benefits program, acted on.

### e08 · score −2
"declares all cryptocurrency transactions illegal" — PBOC circular, Baker McKenzie, Oct 2021 (https://blockchain.bakermckenzie.com/2021/10/04/chinas-central-bank-declares-all-cryptocurrency-transactions-illegal/)
Statement: leave crypto largely alone. Xi's state banned all crypto transactions and mining outright in 2021 and extended the ban to stablecoins in 2026 — the strongest possible disagreement, acted on.

### e09 · score −2
"As many as 80 workers' rights activists were detained" — HRW on the Jasic crackdown (https://www.hrw.org/news/2018/12/03/china-release-workers-student-activists)
US analogue: valuing independent unions. Only the party-run ACFTU is legal; Xi's police jailed and disappeared Jasic worker-organizers and student allies for attempting an autonomous union — sustained action against free unions.

### e10 · score 0
No analogue: Chinese universities charge low state-set tuition and there is no mass student-debt or cancellation debate in Xi's record. Silent, so 0 per rubric.

### e11 · score +1
"peak carbon dioxide emissions before 2030 and achieve carbon neutrality before 2060" — Xi at UNGA, Climate Home News, Sep 2020 (https://www.climatechangenews.com/2020/09/22/xi-jinping-china-will-achieve-carbon-neutrality-2060/)
Statement: aggressive action worth real growth costs. Xi personally pledged the dual-carbon goals, ended overseas coal finance, and built world-leading renewables — but keeps expanding domestic coal with no phase-down timeline. Support, hedged by actions.

### e12 · score 0
Record is genuinely mixed: Xi's government ran multi-trillion-yuan tax-and-fee cuts for businesses to protect jobs, while his common-prosperity rhetoric rejects letting capital enrich itself (context: https://www.cnbc.com/2021/08/18/chinas-xi-emphasizes-common-prosperity-at-finance-economy-meeting.html)
No consistent position on the trickle-down claim as written — corporate relief is framed as employment policy, not worker prosperity via tax cuts.

### s01 · score +1
"reducing non-medically necessary abortions" — NHC policy, HRW, Sep 2021 (https://www.hrw.org/news/2021/09/27/beijing-reduce-non-medically-necessary-abortions)
Abortion is legal, cheap, and ubiquitous in China and Xi has not restricted its legality — but his pronatalist turn now discourages it and banned online abortion-pill sales. Agreement with legality in practice, hedged by the new push.

### s02 · score +1
Legal gender change is possible only after surgery and IDs offer no third option — Transgender people in China, Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transgender_people_in_China)
US analogue: documents list only male/female. Xi's state recognizes strictly binary documents (change allowed only post-surgery), but this is inherited practice he maintains rather than a campaign he drives — agreement, not emphatic.

### s03 · score −2
"access by the general public to firearms is subject to some of the strictest gun control measures in the world" — Gun control in China, Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gun_control_in_China)
Statement: further restrictions violate a fundamental right. Xi maintains a near-total civilian gun ban with gun crimes punishable up to death — the strongest lived rejection of gun ownership as a right.

### s04 · score −2
"domestic security spending grew 34 percent faster than total spending" — Jamestown Foundation (https://jamestown.org/chinas-domestic-security-spending-analysis-available-data/)
US analogue: shifting police funds to social services. Xi spends more on internal "stability maintenance" than on the military, with Xinjiang/Tibet policing above US per-capita levels — sustained expansion of policing over welfare.

### s05 · score +1
"reinforces administration of foreigners who have entered, stayed and worked in China illegally" — China Briefing on the "three illegals" (https://www.china-briefing.com/news/chinas-new-exit-entry-administration-law-targets-illegal-foreigners)
US analogue: find-and-deport policy. China's exit-entry regime detains and deports "three-illegals" foreigners with re-entry bans, though it is routine enforcement rather than a signature Xi campaign — agreement, not emphatic.

### s06 · score −1
Xi's China remains one of the world's most closed immigration systems; the 2020 draft easing permanent residence was shelved after nationalist backlash (context: https://www.china-briefing.com/news/chinas-new-exit-entry-administration-law-targets-illegal-foreigners)
US analogue: expanding legal immigration as a strength. Green-card issuance stays tiny and naturalization near-zero; the one liberalizing draft died — disagreement in practice, hedged because he tolerated proposing it.

### s07 · score −2
"schools would be required to have students study 'Xi Jinping Thought'" — RFA on the Patriotic Education Law, 2023 (https://www.rfa.org/english/news/china/china-patriotic-education-06272023154054.html)
US analogue: schools teaching structural criticism of national institutions. Xi legislates the opposite — mandatory patriotic education at all levels, with "multiple interpretations of history" banned as ideological deviation (Document 9).

### s08 · score −2
"guide the adaptation of religions to socialist society" — Xi's sinicization speech, Lausanne Movement analysis (https://lausanne.org/global-analysis/sinicization-religion-china)
Statement: religion deserves a larger public role. Xi runs the harshest religious crackdown since the Cultural Revolution — Uyghur internment, demolished mosques and crosses, party control of clergy — emphatic, acted-on suppression of independent religion.

### s09 · score +1
"lowered the age requirement for gender-affirming surgery from 20 to 18" — The China Project, May 2022 (http://thechinaproject.com/2022/05/31/china-lowers-minimum-age-to-18-for-gender-reassignment-surgery/)
US analogue: banning minors' gender medicine over parental/medical approval. China bars surgery under 18 and access for youth is severely constrained (family approval required), but a few state hospitals legally offer puberty suppression to minors — restrictive, yet not the categorical ban the statement describes.

### s10 · score −2
"the penalty for a gun crime is death penalty" — and drug trafficking likewise routinely capital: Capital punishment in China, Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_punishment_in_China)
Statement: nationwide legal marijuana. Xi's China executes drug traffickers, criminalizes cannabis totally, and pressures other countries over legalization — maximal opposition, acted on.

### s11 · score +1
"candidates from minority ethnic groups will not receive bonus points" — China Daily on gaokao policy, May 2024 (https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202405/21/WS664bef01a31082fc043c82df.html)
US analogue: strictly group-blind selection. Under Xi, province after province (Henan 2024, Fujian by 2026, Anhui, Hunan, Guizhou) is abolishing ethnic-minority exam preferences in favor of uniform scoring — a real policy direction, though framed technocratically.

### s12 · score −2
"China remained the world's leading executioner … estimated to be in the thousands" — Amnesty International (https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2026/05/death-penalty-in-2025-facts-and-figures/)
Statement: abolish the death penalty. Xi's China executes more people than the rest of the world combined and hides the count as a state secret — the strongest possible practical rejection.

### y01 · score −2
"Party-owned media must hold the family name of the party" — Xi, 2016, SCMP (https://www.scmp.com/news/china/policies-politics/article/1914136/chinas-top-party-mouthpieces-pledge-absolute-loyalty)
US analogue: trusting mainstream media to get facts right. Xi rejects the premise that media report independent facts at all — media exist to "embody the party's will"; independent journalism is punished. Emphatic, acted on.

### y02 · score −2
"'one person one vote' in name … 'rule of the minority elite' in reality" — State Council report on US democracy, CGTN, Dec 2021 (https://news.cgtn.com/news/2021-12-05/China-releases-report-on-U-S-democracy-15JQhkbdjnq/index.html)
US analogue: trusting election administration. Xi permits no competitive elections (his term-limit repeal passed 2,958–2 in a rubber-stamp body) and his government publishes reports deriding US elections as illegitimate — the distrust pole, acted on.

### y03 · score −2
Document 9 lists judicial independence among banned Western concepts — ChinaFile translation (https://www.chinafile.com/document-9-chinafile-translation)
US analogue: politically neutral law enforcement. Xi's courts and police answer to the party by doctrine, and his security purges (Sun Lijun's "political clique") punish disloyalty, not just crime — law enforcement as explicitly political instrument.

### y04 · score +2
"accused of leading a 'political clique' and being disloyal to President Xi Jinping, was jailed for life" — SCMP, Sep 2022 (https://www.scmp.com/news/china/politics/article/3193527/chinese-political-clique-leader-sun-lijun-faces-life-jail)
US analogue: president fires any obstructing official. Xi removes, jails, or purges officials at every level for obstruction or disloyalty through the party discipline apparatus — the statement's maximal form, routinized.

### y05 · score −2
"53 pro-democracy politicians [arrested] for 'subversion'" — HRW on Hong Kong's NSL (https://www.hrw.org/feature/2021/06/25/dismantling-free-society/hong-kong-one-year-after-national-security-law)

### y06 · score −2
"obligated by the Cyberspace Administration to assist … monitoring and removing 'illegal information'" — Internet censorship in China, Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_censorship_in_China)

### y08 · score +2
"US democracy has become 'a game of money politics'" — State Council report, CGTN, Dec 2021 (https://news.cgtn.com/news/2021-12-05/China-releases-report-on-U-S-democracy-15JQhkbdjnq/index.html)

Mixed: Xi's state is rhetorically technocratic (science-driven planning), but he subordinated expertise to loyalty — punishing COVID whistleblowers and ending zero-COVID abruptly against expert sequencing. Deference only when politically convenient.

### y10 · score +2
"expose 'two-faced people' who are disloyal and dishonest to the party" — security-purge directive, SCMP (https://www.scmp.com/news/china/politics/article/3193527/chinese-political-clique-leader-sun-lijun-faces-life-jail)
US analogue: belief in coordinated internal sabotage. Xi officially claims hidden cliques and hostile forces conspire inside his own security organs and ran a years-long "blade inwards" purge on that theory — the deep-state premise, institutionalized.
