# JD Vance — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: verify-1

Scored on his Senate (2023–24) and vice-presidential (2025–26) record: an
economic populist who breaks GOP orthodoxy on tariffs, antitrust, and minimum
wage, fused with uniformly hard-right social positions and the most explicit
executive-supremacy theory in the administration ("stand before the country
like Andrew Jackson"). Verification strengthened two economic scores (Big Tech
breakup, crypto) where his record is emphatic rather than hedged, and moved
unions from mixed to negative — picket-line photo-ops sit atop a 0% AFL-CIO
voting record and the federal collective-bargaining purge he defends.

## Per-question evidence
### e01 · score +2
"We believe that a million cheap, knockoff toasters aren't worth a single American manufacturing job" — RNC acceptance speech, July 2024 (https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jd-vance-trump-vice-president-economy/)
Chief public defender of the 2025 tariff program, explicitly accepting consumer-price costs — repeated, emphatic, acted on.

### e02 · score −2
Opposes single-payer and any federal guarantee; proposed splitting the chronically ill into separate risk pools and defends OBBBA's Medicaid cuts as VP (https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jd-vance-trump-vice-president-economy/)
Consistent stated opposition plus 2025 action against coverage expansion.

### e03 · score −1
No support for a wealth tax anywhere in the record; he voted for and championed the OBBBA tax cuts benefiting large fortunes (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_JD_Vance)
His populism targets universities and "Wall Street barons" rhetorically, not net-worth taxation — disagree, hedged by the anti-elite rhetoric.

### e04 · score −1
"You raise the minimum wage to $20 an hour... that's the kind of rising tide that actually lifts all boats" — NYT interview, quoted by NPR (https://www.npr.org/sections/planet-money/2024/07/23/g-s1-12513/economic-mind-jd-vance)
Openly rejects the libertarian market-only framing on wages — disagree, though he has pushed no federal increase.

### e05 · score +2
"This is one of the most dangerous companies in the world" — on Google, calling for its breakup, Fox interview, March 2024 (https://www.fastcompany.com/91156980/trump-vp-pick-j-d-vance-supports-big-tech-antitrust-crackdown)
Explicitly urged breaking Google up and praised Lina Khan as "the best person within the Biden administration" (https://reason.com/2024/07/16/reminder-j-d-vance-thinks-lina-khan-is-doing-a-great-job/); his allies drive the administration's antitrust posture. Repeated and emphatic — not a hedge.

### e06 · score −1
Pledges no cuts to Social Security and Medicare, aligning with Trump's "not one penny" platform; no privatization advocacy in the record (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_JD_Vance)
Disagrees with carving the program into private accounts, stated rather than acted.

### e07 · score 0
Genuinely mixed. He floated a $5,000-per-child tax credit in Aug 2024 and champions "pro-family" cash support (https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jd-vance-trump-vice-president-economy/), but disparaged universal day-care subsidies as "class war against normal people" and prefers family/church care over federal programs. Cash for parents yes, program-ized leave and child care no — cancels out on the statement as written.

### e08 · score +2
"We fired Gary Gensler — and we're going to fire everyone like him" — Bitcoin 2025 conference, Las Vegas, May 2025 (https://www.notus.org/trump-white-house/jd-vance-crypto-bitcoin-conference-speech)
"With President Trump, crypto finally has a champion and an ally in the White House" (https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jd-vance-bitcoin-event-today-trump-administration-crypto-ties-grow/). A bitcoin-holding VP purging crypto-hostile regulators — emphatic and acted on.

### e09 · score −1
"It's dumb to hand over a lot of power to a union leadership that is aggressively anti-Republican" — explaining his PRO Act opposition (https://onlabor.org/unpacking-jd-vances-labor-record/)
Walked the 2023 UAW picket line, but opposed the PRO Act, cosponsored the TEAM Act unions call company-union enabling, scored 0% with the AFL-CIO, and defends the 2025 federal collective-bargaining strip. Pro-"worker," anti-union-institution — disagree, hedged by the photo-ops.

### e10 · score +2

### e11 · score −2

### e12 · score +1
