# Joe Biden — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: verify-4

Biden's 2026 placement rests on his presidential record (2021–25) plus an
active post-presidency: center-left New Deal economics (pro-union, IRA
climate spending, student-debt cancellation, anti-privatization), mainstream
Democratic social positions, and an emphatic institutionalism — defending
elections, civil servants, expertise, and Social Security against Trump. His
farewell-address "oligarchy" warning is anti-concentrated-wealth rhetoric,
not anti-system insurgency.

## Per-question evidence
### e01 · score −1
"I believe this approach is a major mistake." — Brookings Institution speech, Dec 10, 2024 (https://www.scmp.com/news/us/economy-trade-business/article/3290232/biden-touts-his-economic-legacy-and-calls-trumps-planned-tariffs-major-mistake)
He denounced Trump's universal tariffs as a consumer tax, yet kept Section 301 China tariffs and added a 100% EV tariff — targeted yes, broad no.

### e02 · score +1
"I think health care is not a privilege, it's a right." — final 2020 presidential debate, Oct 22, 2020 (https://time.com/5903204/joe-biden-health-care-debate/)
Backed a public option toward universal coverage but consistently rejected Medicare for All — support hedged short of a full federal guarantee.

### e03 · score +1
Proposed a "25 percent minimum tax on the wealthiest 0.01 percent" — 2024 State of the Union / FY2025 budget (https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2024/03/11/fact-sheet-the-presidents-budget-for-fiscal-year-2025/)
His billionaire minimum income tax (including unrealized gains) is wealth-tax-adjacent and repeatedly proposed, but he never embraced a Warren/Sanders-style net-worth tax.

### e04 · score −2
Ordered a "$15 minimum wage for federal contractors" — Executive Order 14026, Apr 27, 2021 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Order_14026)
Also pushed the $15 federal minimum in the American Rescue Plan; acted directly against market-set wages.

### e05 · score +1
"Capitalism without competition isn't capitalism; it's exploitation." — remarks signing the competition EO, Jul 9, 2021 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Order_14036)
His DOJ/FTC (Kanter, Khan) sued Google, Amazon and Apple, with breakup remedies on the table — but Biden personally stopped short of calling for breakups.

### e06 · score −2
"Social Security is more than a government program. It's a sacred promise." — Chicago ACRD conference, Apr 15, 2025 (https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/joe-biden/biden-trump-social-security-first-post-presidency-speech-rcna201309)
Chose defending Social Security from cuts/privatization as the theme of his first post-presidency speech; lifelong opponent of individual accounts.

### e07 · score +2

Made federal paid leave and subsidized child care centerpiece legislation (Build Back Better); repeated and acted on.

### e08 · score −2

### e09 · score +2

~$370B climate spending plus aggressive EPA rules; acted at scale despite growth/inflation critiques.

### e12 · score −2
