# Nikki Haley — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: new-4

Haley in 2026 is the Republican establishment's standard-bearer in exile: an
orthodox free-market, hawkish conservative who has spent 2024–2026 publicly
attacking Trump's tariffs and fiscal record while accepting election results,
defending institutions, and branding herself on "consensus." That combination
puts her on the right economically and socially but firmly on the
institutionalist half of the y-axis.

## Per-question evidence

### e01 · score −2
"Trump's Proposed Tariff Would Hurt American Workers. And Reignite Inflation." — op-ed title, 2024 (https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/op-ed-nikki-haley-trumps-proposed-tariff-would-hurt-american-workers-and-reignite)
Repeated, emphatic opposition sustained into 2025: she called Trump's India tariffs "counterproductive" and warned they'd "burn" a key alliance (https://thehill.com/business/5437154-us-china-tariffs-india-oil-haley/).

### e02 · score −2
"Haley declined federal funding for Medicaid expansion" — Think Global Health / NewsNation summary of her record (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
Acted against federal health-coverage expansion as governor and campaigns against government-run healthcare; no support anywhere for a federal guarantee.

### e03 · score −1
"reduce income tax rates … make small-business tax relief permanent" — Freedom Plan summary, Tax Policy Center, Nov 2023 (https://taxpolicycenter.org/taxvox/what-nikki-haleys-tax-and-budget-platform)
Her platform is across-the-board tax cutting with no redistribution component; opposition to a wealth tax is implied by the whole program though never litigated by name — hedged −1.

### e04 · score +1
"we are going to be one of the lowest union-participation states in the country" — as SC governor (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Nikki_Haley)
Her record is consistently anti-mandate, low-cost-labor business recruitment and deregulation; no direct minimum-wage quote found, so hedged +1 rather than +2.

### e05 · score 0
Her big-tech agenda targets algorithm transparency and user verification ("social media companies must show their algorithms" — https://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/4309883-haley-urges-social-media-reforms-user-accountability/), but she has never endorsed antitrust breakups. Genuinely silent on the breakup question itself.

### e06 · score 0
Her Social Security fix is raising the retirement age for young workers and chained-CPI COLAs (https://www.cnn.com/2023/12/05/politics/nikki-haley-social-security-medicare-reform/index.html) — real benefit-side reform, but no documented call for private individual accounts. Mixed/silent on privatization as written.

### e07 · score −1
"special interests won't get a penny in taxpayer bailouts" — economic-plan rollout, Sept 2023 (https://www.cnn.com/2023/09/22/politics/nikki-haley-economic-proposal/)
She campaigns against new federal spending programs from both parties; a new federal leave/child-care entitlement runs against her stated program, though she has not addressed it by name — hedged −1.

### e08 · score 0
No findable position on cryptocurrency regulation in her 2023–2026 record; the topic is absent from her Freedom Plan and campaign coverage.

### e09 · score −2
"we are going to be one of the lowest union-participation states in the country" — as governor, recruiting business to SC (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Nikki_Haley)
Acted on it for six years: fought Boeing unionization, championed right-to-work. Emphatic, repeated, acted-on hostility to unions.

### e10 · score +2
"a fantastic win for individual liberty and freedom" — on SCOTUS striking Biden's loan forgiveness, June 2023 (https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/news/20614/nikki-haley-calls-supreme-court-rulings-a-fantastic-win-for-individual-liberty/)
She framed cancellation as "picking winners and losers on whose loans you forgive" — direct, emphatic agreement with the statement's logic.

### e11 · score −2
"pledged to again withdraw the U.S. from the Paris Agreement, revoke regulations restricting fossil fuel production" — 2024 platform summary (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
Accepts human-caused climate change but emphatically rejects costly federal action — the statement as written she strongly rejects.

### e12 · score +1
"corporate tax cuts were made permanent … small businesses did not receive the same treatment" — Freedom Plan framing, Sept 2023 (https://www.cnn.com/2023/09/22/politics/nikki-haley-economic-proposal/)
Treats the 2017 corporate cuts as a pro-worker success to be extended to small business; supportive but her emphasis is small-business relief, so +1.

### s01 · score −1
"if she were still governor … she would sign a 6-week ban" — Nov 2023 interview, via Ballotpedia summary (https://ballotpedia.org/Nikki_Haley_presidential_campaign,_2024)
Pro-life, signed a 20-week SC ban, would sign a 6-week ban — but pushes "national consensus," rules out criminalizing women, and opposes a federal ban push. Disagrees with the statement, hedged by her softer national posture.

### s02 · score +1
Calls trans girls in women's sports "the women's issue of our time" (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
Firm two-sex framing in sports and schools, but she opposed SC's bathroom bill as unnecessary and avoids documents/ID specifics — agree, hedged.

### s03 · score +2
"signed reciprocity legislation … expanded concealed carry rights, and backed the Constitutional Carry Act" — record summary (https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2023/presidential-candidates-2024-policies-issues/nikki-haley-guns-crime/)
Acted repeatedly as governor to expand gun rights; campaigns as a Second Amendment defender against new restrictions.

### s04 · score −1
Her crime platform is back-the-blue funding and tougher enforcement (https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2023/presidential-candidates-2024-policies-issues/nikki-haley-guns-crime/)
Consistently opposes the defund/redirect frame; scored −1 rather than −2 because her statements target "defunding" rather than mental-health co-response specifically.

### s05 · score +1
"proposed re-implementing … Migrant Protection Protocols … and Title 42" — 2024 platform (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
Signed an "Arizona-style" enforcement law in 2011 and backs deporting people here illegally, but without the "whatever it takes" maximalism — she has criticized indiscriminate tactics and framed enforcement as rule-of-law.

### s06 · score +1
"we should [n]ever ban anyone based on their religion … un-American" — on the Muslim ban (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Nikki_Haley)
Daughter of legal immigrants who campaigns on merit-based legal immigration as a strength (https://www.nbcnews.com/meet-the-press/meetthepressblog/nikki-haley-stands-key-issues-rcna70838); supports it hedged — she'd tie any expansion to border enforcement first.

### s07 · score −2
"America is not a racist country" — repeated 2023–24 campaign line, launch video and town halls (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
A signature, repeated theme: she rejects the systemic-racism frame outright and opposes teaching it.

### s08 · score 0
Her Christian faith is publicly prominent (Charleston, campaign speeches), but no documented policy push to enlarge religion's role in public life was found; the record is personal-faith rhetoric, not programmatic.

### s09 · score 0
"You shouldn't allow a child to have a gender-changing procedure until … 18" vs. "the law should stay out of it, and I think parents should handle it" — June 2023 CBS interview, both via (https://www.abc27.com/news/desantis-accuses-haley-of-caving-on-gender-affirming-care/amp/)
Genuinely contradictory on the statement as written: opposes minor transition, but explicitly rejected state bans overriding parents — the exact scenario the question poses. DeSantis attacked her from the right for it.

### s10 · score 0
No findable 2023–2026 statement on nationwide marijuana legalization; the issue is absent from her platform coverage.

### s11 · score +2
"Picking winners and losers based on race is fundamentally wrong" — statement on the SFFA ruling, June 2023 (https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/statement-nikki-haley-the-supreme-court-affirmative-action-ruling)
Also called affirmative-action policies "un-American" and "racist" (https://www.foxnews.com/politics/affirmative-action-policies-un-american-racist-nikki-haley-says-supreme-court-hears-arguments) — repeated, emphatic agreement with strict race-blindness.

### s12 · score −2
"Urged prosecutors to seek death penalty against Dylann Roof" — as governor, 2015 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_positions_of_Nikki_Haley)
Acted to pursue capital punishment; nothing in her record supports abolition — strong disagreement with the statement.

### y01 · score 0
She complains of media bias in campaign mode but is a fixture of mainstream outlets (op-eds, Sunday shows) and has never run an anti-press campaign; the record is genuinely mixed on whether media get basic facts right.

### y02 · score +2
"Do I think Joe Biden is the legitimate president? … Yes." — 2022, via WaPo tracker (https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2023/presidential-candidates-2024-policies-issues/nikki-haley-jan-6-2020-election-results/)
Repeatedly affirmed Biden's win and hit Trump's election lies on the debate stage (https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2024/01/11/nikki-haley-january-6-republican-debate/).

### y03 · score −1
DOJ and FBI have "lost all credibility with the American people" and need a "complete overhaul" — 2023 (https://www.newsweek.com/nikki-haley-harshest-words-trump-1806129)
Called the NY Trump prosecution "political" but conceded the documents case showed Trump "incredibly reckless" — critical of DOJ fairness, but not a weaponization crusader; hedged −1.

### y04 · score +1
"No bureaucrat should hold the same position for more than five years" — campaign proposal (https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/haley-campaign-press-release-nikki-haley-the-establishments-worst-nightmare)
Wants churn and accountability in the civil service, but frames it as term limits, not at-will presidential firing — agree, hedged.

### y05 · score −1
January 6 was "a terrible day" and Trump "will have to answer for it" (https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2023/presidential-candidates-2024-policies-issues/nikki-haley-jan-6-2020-election-results/)
A law-and-order institutionalist who condemned the era's marquee act of political disruption; no sympathy anywhere for disruptive protest tactics.

### y06 · score −1
"every person on social media should be verified by their name … a national security threat" — Nov 2023 (https://www.cbsnews.com/news/nikki-haley-name-verification-social-media-profiles/)
She proposed direct government mandates on platforms and walked it back only partially — she plainly does not hold the never-pressure-platforms absolutist position.

### y07 · score +1
"a fantastic win for individual liberty" — celebrating SCOTUS rulings, June 2023 (https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/news/20614/nikki-haley-calls-supreme-court-rulings-a-fantastic-win-for-individual-liberty/)
Treats the Court as legitimate and authoritative; no record of urging defiance of rulings she dislikes — institutional respect, stated rather than tested, so +1.

### y08 · score 0
She slams "the establishment" and both parties' spending (https://www.cnn.com/2023/09/22/politics/nikki-haley-economic-proposal/) yet ran the donor-class 2024 campaign par excellence; genuinely mixed on the donors-over-voters claim.

### y09 · score −1
Acknowledges human-caused climate change but "rejected policies designed to curb it" (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
Accepts expert findings selectively while refusing the policy deference the statement describes — disagree, hedged.

### y10 · score 0
She attacks agency "credibility" but has never claimed a coordinated internal plot against presidents; she ran against candidates making that claim. Mixed/silent on the conspiracy framing itself.

### y11 · score −1
Her reform list is term limits and competency tests (https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/haley-campaign-press-release-nikki-haley-the-establishments-worst-nightmare) — incremental fixes defended within the constitutional order she celebrates; she'd reject "fundamental restructuring." Hedged for lack of a direct quote.

### y12 · score +2
Called for "national consensus" on abortion and campaigned on civility across her 2024 run (https://www.newsnationnow.com/politics/2024-election/nikki-haley-political-views-2024/)
Consensus-and-compromise is her core brand — the trait rivals attacked her for (https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/magazine-features/2906089/why-haley-lost-republicans-arent-in-the-mood-for-civility/).
