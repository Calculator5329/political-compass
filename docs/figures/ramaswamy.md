# Vivek Ramaswamy — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: new-4

Ramaswamy in 2026 is the GOP nominee-apparent for Ohio governor, running on
abolishing the state income tax, rolling back property taxes, and merit-based
schools — a supply-side program layered over the national brand he built in
2023–24: shut down the FBI and Department of Education, fire 75% of the
federal workforce, "climate change agenda is a hoax," Jan 6 "an inside job."
That record makes him hard-right on x and among the most insurgent figures on
the board on y — anti-agency, anti-media, anti-expert — while remaining
crypto-libertarian and pro-marijuana rather than uniformly authoritarian.

## Per-question evidence

### e01 · score 0
He wrote that he "disapproved of [Trump's] large-scale government spending and his tariff policies" (https://www.cnn.com/2023/08/31/politics/fact-check-vivek-ramaswamy-book-trump-2020-election/index.html), yet since 2024 has campaigned as a full-spectrum Trump ally demanding economic "independence from China." Genuinely contradictory on broad tariffs as written.

### e02 · score −2
Pledged to shut down the CDC and gut the health bureaucracy — "literally shutting down the administrative state" (https://www.foxnews.com/media/vivek-ramaswamy-vows-gut-several-agencies-including-fbi-irs-cdc)
A candidate dismantling federal health agencies and running on free-market, anti-entitlement-expansion economics stands emphatically against a federal insurance guarantee.

### e03 · score −1
A billionaire founder who "denounced business strategies being dictated by … social justice concerns" and defends wealth creation (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vivek_Ramaswamy)
No direct wealth-tax quote found, but his anti-redistribution, capital-gains-cutting program (https://www.statenews.org/government-politics/2026-07-14/ramaswamy-touts-income-capital-gains-property-tax-cuts-as-ohios-main-business-group-endorses-him) makes opposition clear — hedged −1.

### e04 · score +1
Campaigns on deregulation and market wages; his Ohio agenda is tax and regulatory relief, not wage mandates (https://www.statenews.org/government-politics/2026-04-06/ohio-governor-race-republican-ramaswamy-talks-zero-income-taxes-property-tax-rollback)
Consistent free-market position; hedged +1 for lack of a minimum-wage-specific quote.

### e05 · score 0
He says "big tech stole the 2020 election" (https://www.cnn.com/2023/08/31/politics/fact-check-vivek-ramaswamy-book-trump-2020-election/index.html) yet wants regulators reined in, not empowered; his remedy is ending government-tech collusion, not antitrust breakups. Mixed as written.

### e06 · score +1
Supports raising the retirement age for younger workers and giving individuals "more control over their retirement savings" (https://www.isidewith.com/candidates/vivek-ramaswamy/policies/domestic-policy/social-security), while promising "No cuts for seniors on Social Security or Medicare" (https://www.axios.com/2024/12/04/vivek-ramaswamy-doge-entitlement-spending-cuts) — supportive of partial-privatization direction, hedged.

### e07 · score 0
No findable position on federal paid leave or child care; his family-policy rhetoric is cultural ("nuclear family"), not programmatic.

### e08 · score +2
Crypto policy framework centered on "affirming self-custody rights and reining in federal regulators" — Nov 2023 (https://www.axios.com/2023/11/17/vivek-ramaswamy-crypto)
Published a whole deregulatory crypto platform; emphatic agreement with leaving crypto largely alone.

### e09 · score −1

Acted-on hostility to public unions; −1 rather than −2 because he has courted private-sector union workers rhetorically.

### e10 · score +1
Pledged to abolish the Department of Education and opposes federal loan bailouts as part of that agenda (https://www.nbcnews.com/meet-the-press/meetthepressblog/vivek-ramaswamy-lays-plans-eliminate-federal-agencies-fbi-rcna95409)
Clear opposition to cancellation; hedged +1 since the found quotes target the department rather than forgiveness's fairness per se.

### e11 · score −2

### e12 · score +1
