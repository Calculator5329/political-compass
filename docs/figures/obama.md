# Barack Obama — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: verify-4

Obama in 2026 is the Democratic Party's chief institutionalist elder:
center-left market-friendly economics (ACA, Paris, Buffett Rule — never a
wealth tax), mainstream liberal social positions, and a post-presidency
devoted to defending elections, courts, universities, the press, and free
speech against Trump ("imagine if I had done any of this," Hamilton College
2025). His critiques of Trump are framed as defense of the constitutional
order, placing him deep in the institutionalist half.

## Per-question evidence
### e01 · score −2
Negotiated and championed the Trans-Pacific Partnership as president — TPP advocacy, 2015–16 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-Pacific_Partnership)
A career free-trader who fought his own base for TPP and warned protectionism means "less trade... higher prices"; no recent reversal.

### e02 · score +1
"Health care is not a privilege for the fortunate few — it is a right." — statement on ACA anniversary framing, repeated 2013–17 (https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/remarks-with-former-president-barack-obama-the-patient-protection-and-affordable-care-act)
Signed the ACA — the biggest coverage expansion in generations — but bargained away the public option and rejected single-payer as impractical; support hedged short of a full guarantee.

### e03 · score 0

Genuinely mixed on the statement as written: he repeatedly backed higher income taxes on the rich but never endorsed a net-worth tax, and declined to back the Warren/Sanders wealth-tax plans.
