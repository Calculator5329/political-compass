# Vladimir Putin — evidence dossier
Updated: 2026-07-18 · Researcher: verify-10

Scored per METHOD.md's non-US-figures rule: every US-framed statement is answered
by the closest analogue in Putin's own governance record, noted per item. His 2026
placement is driven by statist-protectionist economics (import substitution,
state capture of tech firms, decreed minimum-wage hikes, 2024 tax increases on
high incomes and corporations, huge pronatalist subsidies), enforced
"traditional values" social policy, and the authoritarian pole on nearly every
system item — rigged elections, subordinated courts, crushed protest and media,
term limits reset by amendment.

## Per-question evidence

### e01 · score +2
"achieved very serious and positive results" — Putin on import substitution, TASS, 2020 (https://tass.com/economy/1091317)
US analogue: tariffs to protect domestic industry. Putin's counter-sanctions embargo and decade-long import-substitution / "technological sovereignty" program is exactly this, accepting higher consumer prices; sustained and acted on.

### e02 · score +1
"healthcare in state and municipal health institutions… must be free" — Russian Constitution Art. 41, via Healthcare in Russia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Healthcare_in_Russia)
US analogue: government-guaranteed insurance. Putin maintains universal compulsory OMS coverage, but his "optimization" era underfunded it and out-of-pocket costs are high — support in structure, hedged in practice.

### e03 · score 0
"a law on introducing a progressive personal income tax scale" — Kremlin.ru, Jul 2024 (http://en.kremlin.ru/acts/news/74542)
US analogue: wealth tax on large fortunes. Putin defended a flat 13% tax for two decades, then signed a progressive scale (to 22%) and a windfall tax — yet oligarch fortunes stay untaxed as wealth. Genuinely mixed record; no wealth-tax analogue either way.

### e04 · score −2
"minimum wage from January 1, 2026 set at 27,093 rubles" — Interfax, Nov 2025 (https://interfax.com/newsroom/top-stories/115020/)
Statement says the market, not government, should set the minimum wage. Putin repeatedly signs statutory MROT hikes (20.7% for 2026) and ordered it doubled by 2030 — emphatic state wage-setting, acted on annually.

### e05 · score +2
"Yandex sells assets to state-run VK" — Euronews, Aug 2022 (https://www.euronews.com/next/2022/08/23/russia-internet-yandex)
US analogue: breaking up the largest tech firms. Putin's state forcibly dismembered and captured Yandex (news/Zen stripped to Kremlin-aligned VK, company split in 2023–24) — the strongest form of acting to break dominant tech companies to state ends.

### e06 · score −2
"6% … were simply redirected to payments for current pensioners" — Pension Policy International (https://www.pensionpolicyinternational.com/russia-froze-the-national-welfare-fund-pensions-of-russian-citizens-under-threat/)
US analogue: partial privatization of Social Security into individual accounts. Putin did the opposite: froze the funded individual-account component in 2014 and has extended the freeze ever since, folding contributions back into the state PAYG system.

### e07 · score +2
"payments reached 630,400 rubles for a first child" — Moscow Times, Oct 2024 (https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2024/10/14/russia-to-raise-maternity-subsidies-in-2025-amid-population-crisis-a86676)
US analogue: federal family-support programs. Putin's signature maternity-capital program (extended to 2030 during his campaign), plus his "Year of the Family," is a massive federal cash program for parents — repeated, emphatic, acted on.

### e08 · score −1
"permitting digital currency payments for international trade" — CNBC, Jul 2024 (https://www.cnbc.com/2024/07/30/russia-considers-legalizing-crypto-as-a-form-of-payment-amid-sanctions.html)
Statement: government should leave crypto alone. Putin's 2024 laws keep the domestic payment ban, license miners, and put cross-border use under central-bank oversight — regulate-and-harness, not laissez-faire; hedged by his new pro-mining tilt.

### e09 · score −2
"pressure and persecution including even imprisonment … against trade union activists" — European Lawyers for Workers Network (https://elw-network.eu/freedom-of-association-of-trade-unions-in-modern-russia-practice-and-challenges/)
US analogue: valuing independent unions. Putin's system co-opts the official FNPR, makes lawful strikes nearly impossible, and represses independent unions with foreign-agent labels and prosecutions — sustained action against them.

### e10 · score 0
No analogue: Russian higher education is largely state-funded with budget places; a US-style student-debt-cancellation debate does not exist in Putin's record. Silent, so 0 per rubric.

### e11 · score −2
"focuses almost exclusively on promoting fossil fuel extraction, consumption, and exports" — Climate Action Tracker on Russia's Energy Strategy (https://climateactiontracker.org/countries/russian-federation/)
Statement: aggressive climate action worth real economic costs. Putin's paper 2060 pledge is rated "critically insufficient"; he doubles down on Vostok oil, coal, and gas — repeatedly acting against costly climate action.

### e12 · score −2
"headline rate of corporate tax will rise to 25 percent" — Croner-i on 2024 reform (https://library.croneri.co.uk/wkus-gdn01-gdn01167538)
Statement: cutting corporate taxes helps workers. Putin signed the 2024 hike of profit tax from 20% to 25% plus a 2023 windfall tax on big business to fund the war-era budget — acted directly against corporate tax cuts.

### s01 · score 0
"Russia limits women's access to abortion, citing demographic changes" — Al Jazeera, Nov 2023 (https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2023/11/28/russia-limits-womens-access-to-abortion-citing-demographic-changes)
Abortion stays legal on request to 12 weeks and Putin has resisted a federal ban, but his state squeezes access (private-clinic bans, "childfree propaganda" law, pressure campaigns). Genuinely mixed: legal in most circumstances, actively discouraged.

### s02 · score +2
"adoption of transphobic legislation a horrendous blow" — Amnesty on 2023 law (https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2023/07/russia-adoption-of-transphobic-legislation-a-horrendous-blow-to-human-rights/)
US analogue: IDs list only male or female. Putin signed the July 2023 law banning legal gender change and transition care outright — the maximal version of a two-sexes documents policy, acted on.

### s03 · score −2
"raises the minimum age for buying hunting rifles … from 18 to 21" — RFE/RL, Jun 2021 (https://www.rferl.org/a/russia-gun-ownership-law/31331101.html)
Statement: further gun restrictions violate a fundamental right. Russia licenses narrowly, bans handguns, and Putin signed post-Kazan tightening — he treats gun ownership as a revocable privilege and repeatedly restricts it.

### s04 · score −2
"created to give Putin more direct authority to control protests" — CSIS on Rosgvardiya (https://www.csis.org/blogs/post-soviet-post/rosgvardiya-hurtling-towards-confrontation)
US analogue: redirecting police funding to social services. Putin built the opposite: a 340,000-strong internal-security National Guard under a personal loyalist, deployed against protests — sustained expansion of policing over social spending.

### s05 · score +2
"signed a decree requiring migrants illegally present in Russia to leave" — Global Detention Project / Moscow Times reporting, 2024–25 (https://www.globaldetentionproject.org/russia-weaponising-immigration-policies-to-push-migrants-into-war-and-launching-a-new-expulsion-regime)
US analogue: find-and-deport whatever it takes. Putin signed the court-free "expulsion regime," a registry of ~685,000 "controlled persons," and doubled deportations to 80,000+ in 2024 — emphatic, acted on.

### s06 · score −1
"new migration regulations 'very dangerous' for millions of foreign laborers" — Moscow Times, Feb 2025 (https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2025/02/05/russias-new-migration-regulations-very-dangerous-for-millions-of-foreign-laborers-a86344)
US analogue: expanding legal immigration. Putin concedes Russia's war economy needs migrant labor yet keeps tightening legal channels and rights for migrants — opposition in practice, hedged by economic need.

### s07 · score −2
"a consolidated instructional methodology in 'historical education' … from kindergarten" — russiapost.info on Putin's May 2024 decree (https://russiapost.info/society/russian_education)
US analogue: schools teaching structural criticism of the nation's institutions. Putin mandates the opposite — unified patriotic history textbooks, "Fundamentals of Russian Statehood," criminalized "falsification of history" — repeated state action against critical curricula.

### s08 · score +2
"A special role … belongs to Orthodoxy" — Decree 809 on traditional values, Nov 2022 (https://cne.news/article/2008-russian-president-signs-decree-on-traditional-values)
US analogue: larger public role for religion. Putin decreed Orthodox-rooted "traditional spiritual-moral values" as state policy and governs in open alliance with the Church — emphatic and acted on.

### s09 · score +2
"transphobic legislation … banning gender-affirming care" — Amnesty, Jul 2023 (https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2023/07/russia-adoption-of-transphobic-legislation-a-horrendous-blow-to-human-rights/)
Statement asks about banning minors' care even over parental/medical approval; Putin's 2023 law bans transition care for everyone, minors included, regardless of consent — beyond the statement's maximal position.

### s10 · score −2
"[I am] strongly against the legalization of marijuana" — Putin, 2013, via Sensi Seeds law review (https://sensiseeds.com/en/blog/countries/cannabis-in-russia-laws-use-history/)
Direct quote plus governing record: cannabis fully criminalized, no medical program, Russia campaigns internationally against legalization. Repeated, emphatic opposition.

### s11 · score +1
"derided … cancel culture, reverse racism and the 'monstrous' forcing of transgenderism" — Washington Post on Valdai 2021 (https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2021/10/22/putin-valdai-speech-trump-cancel-culture/)
US analogue: rejecting race/gender-conscious hiring. Putin rhetorically scorns Western diversity mandates as "reverse racism," but has no domestic hiring-policy record on the question — stated, rhetorical only.

### s12 · score +1
"the Kremlin was not involved in the calls to restore the death penalty" — Peskov via US News, Mar 2024 (https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2024-03-25/kremlin-we-are-not-joining-death-penalty-debate-after-concert-hall-attack)
Russia's moratorium has held through Putin's entire tenure, and he declined to lift it even after Crocus when allies demanded it — supports abolition in practice, but never formally abolished; hedged.

### y01 · score −2
"Disrupted, Throttled, and Blocked: State Censorship … in Russia" — HRW, Jul 2025 (https://www.hrw.org/report/2025/07/30/disrupted-throttled-and-blocked/state-censorship-control-and-increasing-isolation)
US analogue: trusting mainstream media. Putin brands independent and Western outlets liars/foreign agents and has destroyed or blocked them (Meta "extremist," YouTube throttled) — total, acted-on distrust of non-state media.

### y02 · score −2
"was neither free nor fair" — US Mission to OSCE on Russia's 2024 election (https://osce.usmission.gov/on-the-russian-presidential-elections-and-russias-violations-of-osce-principles-and-commitments/)
US analogue: trusting election administration. Putin runs elections with jailed rivals and barred observers, and publicly pushes fraud narratives about US elections (Carlson interview) — the anti-trust pole, acted on.

### y03 · score −2
"the persecution of a political rival for political reasons" — Putin on Trump cases, PBS, Sep 2023 (https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/putin-says-prosecution-of-trump-shows-rottenness-u-s-political-system)
He explicitly calls US federal law enforcement politically weaponized, and his own prosecutors run political cases (Navalny) — emphatic disagreement with the statement from both directions.

### y04 · score +2
"the right of the president to remove federal justices" — 2020 amendments, Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_amendments_to_the_Constitution_of_Russia)
US analogue: president fires any obstructing official. Putin's "power vertical" lets him dismiss governors, ministers, and now initiate removal of judges — constitutionalized, maximal version of the statement.

### y05 · score −2
"80 people in 20 cities … arrested for election-related offences" — OVD-Info via OSCE reporting, 2024 (https://osce.usmission.gov/on-the-russian-presidential-elections-and-russias-violations-of-osce-principles-and-commitments/)
Statement: mass civil disobedience is justified. Putin criminalizes even single-picket protest and has Rosgvardiya break up demonstrations — repeated, emphatic action against the statement.

### y06 · score −2
"Russia court bans Facebook, Instagram over 'extremism'" — PBS, Mar 2022 (https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/russia-court-bans-facebook-instagram-over-extremism-charges)
Statement: government should never pressure platforms over lawful speech. Putin's state does not merely pressure platforms — it bans, throttles, and fines them for lawful content via Roskomnadzor.

### y07 · score −2
"allowing Russia to ignore criminal rulings of foreign and international courts" — Moscow Times, Dec 2025 (https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2025/12/29/putin-signs-law-allowing-russia-to-ignore-criminal-rulings-of-foreign-and-international-courts-a91569)
US analogue: respecting high-court rulings you dislike. Putin left the ECHR, legislated a right to ignore international judgments, dismisses the ICC warrant, and gained power to remove domestic judges — courts bind him only when convenient.

### y08 · score +2
"the rottenness of the American political system" — Putin, PBS, Sep 2023 (https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/putin-says-prosecution-of-trump-shows-rottenness-u-s-political-system)
He consistently portrays Western party democracy as a stage-managed elite/donor game ("elites' mindsets" captured US policy, per the Carlson interview) — repeated, emphatic agreement with the cynical framing.

### y09 · score −1
"technological breakthroughs at the scale … envisioned by the Kremlin being 'virtually impossible'" — New Eurasian Strategies Centre (https://nestcentre.org/russian-technological-sovereignty/)
Mixed but tilting anti-expert: he retains technocrats (central bank) yet overrides economic, climate, and epidemiological consensus for political goals and has criminalized independent expertise. Disagrees, hedged.

### y10 · score +2
"US foreign policy decisions had been captured by … 'elites' mindsets'" — Chatham House on Putin's Carlson interview, Feb 2024 (https://www.chathamhouse.org/2024/02/putins-carlson-interview-shows-links-between-trump-talk-and-russian-messaging)
Putin repeatedly endorses the deep-state narrative: US presidents (Trump specifically) are undercut and sabotaged by permanent bureaucracies and elites — a sustained, public theme.

### y11 · score +2
"Putin signs constitutional changes that allow him to rule until 2036" — RFE/RL, Apr 2021 (https://www.rferl.org/a/putin-signs-law-to-rule-until-2036/31187934.html)

### y12 · score −2

US analogue: cross-party compromise as the system working. Putin eliminated real opposition rather than bargain with it, and offers only maximalist terms abroad — compromise treated as weakness, repeatedly acted against.
